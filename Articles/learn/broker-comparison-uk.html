Hargreaves Lansdown vs AJ Bell vs Trading 212 vs Freetrade: Which UK Broker is Best in 2026?

Updated July 2026 · 10 min read · Suitable for all investors

Choosing the right investment platform is one of the most important financial decisions a UK investor can make — and one of the most underappreciated. Most investors spend hours researching individual stocks but minutes choosing the platform where they will hold them for decades.

The reason broker choice matters so much is straightforward: platform fees compound just like returns, but in the wrong direction. A 0.5% annual platform fee difference on a £100,000 portfolio is £500 per year. Over 20 years, with reinvested growth, that difference can amount to tens of thousands of pounds in foregone wealth. Choosing the right platform for your portfolio size, investment style, and tax wrapper needs is a genuine investment decision.

This guide compares the five major UK investment platforms as of mid-2026: Hargreaves Lansdown, AJ Bell, Trading 212, Freetrade, and Vanguard. We cover fees, account types, tools, and the practical recommendation for different investor profiles.

Why Platform Fees Matter More Than You Think

To understand why broker comparison is worth your time, consider this illustration:

Portfolio: £100,000 growing at 8% per year Platform A: 0.25% annual fee → After 20 years: £413,000 Platform B: 0.75% annual fee → After 20 years: £349,000 Difference: £64,000 — 18% of final portfolio value On a £500,000 portfolio: Platform A: 0.25% = £1,250/year Platform B: 0.75% = £3,750/year Annual difference: £2,500 — compounding against you

These are not trivial amounts. The power of compounding works against you when fees compound too. Choosing the right platform for your portfolio size is not financial pedantry — it is one of the highest-value decisions you can make as a long-term investor.

Hargreaves Lansdown (HL)

Hargreaves Lansdown is the UK's largest investment platform by client assets, with approximately 1.6 million clients and over £150 billion in assets under administration as of 2026. It is the market leader by brand recognition and arguably by quality of research and service.

Fees

  • Platform fee (shares/ETFs/investment trusts): 0.45% per year, capped at £45 per year. Once your share portfolio reaches £10,000, you are paying the maximum £45/year regardless of portfolio size. This makes HL extremely cost-effective for large share portfolios.
  • Platform fee (funds): 0.45% per year, capped at £45/year for the first £250,000, then 0.25% on the next £750,000, then 0.10% above £1m.
  • Dealing fee (shares/ETFs): £11.95 per trade for fewer than 10 trades/month. Drops to £8.95 (10-19 trades) and £5.95 (20+ trades). Free for fund deals.
  • FX fee (US/international stocks): 1.0% on the first £5,000 of the trade, reducing to 0.75% and lower on larger amounts.

Account Types

ISA, SIPP, GIA (General Investment Account), LISA (Lifetime ISA), Junior ISA, Junior SIPP, Junior GIA. HL offers the broadest range of tax wrappers of any major UK platform.

Strengths

  • Excellent research tools, company analysis, and fund ratings
  • Premium telephone service with knowledgeable client support team
  • Capped platform fee extremely cost-effective for large share portfolios
  • Most trusted brand in UK retail investing
  • Broad investment universe: UK and international shares, funds, ETFs, investment trusts, bonds

Weaknesses

  • High dealing charges (£11.95) punish frequent traders and small portfolios
  • High FX fee (1%) makes US stock investing expensive
  • No fractional shares

Best for: Investors with large share portfolios (over £100,000) who value premium research, service quality, and account type breadth. The capped platform fee makes HL genuinely competitive at scale.

AJ Bell

AJ Bell is the UK's second-largest retail investment platform with approximately 500,000 clients. Listed on the LSE (AJB), AJ Bell offers a strong combination of competitive fees, solid tools, and good account variety — making it the natural alternative to HL for cost-conscious investors.

Fees

  • Platform fee (shares/ETFs): 0.25% per year, capped at £3.50 per month (£42/year). For investors with share portfolios above £16,800, the cap kicks in.
  • Platform fee (funds): 0.25% (capped at £10/month for the first £250,000; reducing above that).
  • Dealing fee: £9.95 per share/ETF trade. £4.95 for frequent traders (10+ trades in previous month). £1.50 for funds.
  • FX fee: 0.75% on international trades.

Account Types

ISA, SIPP, GIA, LISA, Junior ISA, Junior SIPP.

Strengths

  • Lower platform fee than HL, with a low cap
  • Good app and web interface
  • Solid research and screening tools
  • Full range of tax wrappers

Weaknesses

  • Dealing fees higher than commission-free platforms
  • Less comprehensive research than HL
  • No fractional shares

Best for: Investors with medium-to-large portfolios (£50,000-£500,000) who want solid service, good tools, and lower fees than HL. AJ Bell is particularly attractive for SIPP holders who want more active management than a pure passive platform like Vanguard.

Trading 212

Trading 212 has grown rapidly to become one of the most popular platforms among UK retail investors, particularly younger investors. Its defining feature is commission-free trading — no dealing fees on any trade.

Fees

  • Platform fee: None.
  • Dealing fee: Zero. Commission-free on all UK and international stocks.
  • FX fee: 0.15% on international trades — the lowest of any major UK platform. This makes Trading 212 the best value platform for investors buying US stocks.
  • ISA fee: None.

Account Types

ISA and GIA only. No SIPP, no LISA, no Junior ISA.

Strengths

  • Zero dealing fees — ideal for frequent traders
  • Lowest FX fee of any major UK platform (0.15%)
  • Fractional shares from £1 — can invest small amounts in any stock
  • Clean, fast app
  • Interest paid on uninvested cash

Weaknesses

  • No SIPP — cannot use for pension investing
  • Limited research tools compared to HL or AJ Bell
  • Smaller customer support team than established incumbents
  • Makes money primarily through the bid-ask spread on its order routing, which is not always fully transparent

Best for: Beginners and small-portfolio investors, frequent traders, US stock investors who want the lowest FX fees, and investors building outside of pension wrappers. Not suitable as a sole platform for investors who need a SIPP.

Freetrade

Freetrade positioned itself as the UK's first truly commission-free platform when it launched in 2018. It remains popular with cost-conscious investors, particularly for its clean interface and growing stock universe.

Fees

  • Basic plan: Free. Access to a GIA with commission-free trades.
  • Standard plan (£4.99/month): Adds ISA access and premium customer support.
  • Plus plan (£9.99/month): Adds SIPP, extended trading hours, and additional features.
  • FX fee: 0.99% on international trades — higher than Trading 212.

Account Types

GIA (free), ISA (Standard plan), SIPP (Plus plan).

Strengths

  • Clean, simple interface ideal for beginners
  • Commission-free trading
  • SIPP available (on Plus plan) at relatively low cost
  • Good for straightforward long-term investors

Weaknesses

  • Monthly subscription model means costs add up over time
  • Higher FX fee than Trading 212
  • Smaller stock universe than HL or AJ Bell
  • Limited research and screening tools

Best for: Cost-conscious beginners who want commission-free trading with a SIPP at a low monthly cost. The Plus plan at £9.99/month (£120/year) is competitive for a SIPP if you make frequent trades that would otherwise incur dealing fees.

Vanguard

Vanguard's platform is unique: it only allows investment in Vanguard's own funds and ETFs. You cannot buy individual stocks, non-Vanguard ETFs, or any other investment. But for pure passive index investors, Vanguard offers the lowest platform costs available in the UK.

Fees

  • Platform fee: 0.15% per year, capped at £375/year (reached at £250,000).
  • Dealing fee: None for regular investing; £7.50 for single trades.

Account Types

ISA, SIPP, GIA, Junior ISA, Junior SIPP.

Best for: Investors who want a pure passive, low-cost index investing strategy and are happy to invest exclusively in Vanguard products (e.g., FTSE All-World ETF, UK Government Bond Index Fund). Not suitable for investors who want to pick individual stocks.

FX Fees: The Hidden Cost of International Investing

If you invest in US or international stocks, the FX fee is critical — yet many investors overlook it entirely. When you buy US shares, your platform converts GBP to USD and charges a percentage for doing so. This fee applies every time you buy and every time you sell.

Example: £10,000 in Apple (AAPL), bought and sold once Hargreaves Lansdown (1.0% FX fee): Buy: £10,000 × 1.0% = £100 Sell: £10,000 × 1.0% = £100 Total FX cost: £200 (plus £23.90 in dealing fees) Trading 212 (0.15% FX fee): Buy: £10,000 × 0.15% = £15 Sell: £10,000 × 0.15% = £15 Total FX cost: £30 (no dealing fees) Saving with Trading 212: £193 on a single round-trip trade

For active US stock investors, the FX fee difference between platforms is often larger than the platform fee itself. Trading 212's 0.15% FX fee is genuinely industry-leading and represents a material cost advantage for investors with significant US exposure.

Our Recommendation Framework

  • Portfolio under £20,000, no SIPP needed: Trading 212. Zero fees, fractional shares, excellent FX rates. The best cost structure for smaller portfolios.
  • Portfolio under £20,000, SIPP needed: Freetrade Plus (£9.99/month). Commission-free with SIPP access at a predictable monthly cost.
  • Portfolio £20,000-£100,000: AJ Bell. Competitive platform fee, solid tools, full account type range, better value than HL at this scale.
  • Portfolio over £100,000 (shares-focused): Hargreaves Lansdown. The capped £45/year platform fee makes it exceptionally cost-effective for large share portfolios. Premium research and service quality justified at this level.
  • Pure passive index investor: Vanguard. Lowest platform costs available, full wrapper range, exclusively Vanguard funds.
  • Frequent US stock buyer (any size): Consider Trading 212 for US exposure specifically, even if you hold a primary account elsewhere. The FX fee saving justifies maintaining a second account.
